# Community Bootstrap

A protocol launches once. A community compounds forever. Build it before you need it.

## The Founding Community Principle

The worst time to build a community is the week before launch. The best time was 3 months ago.

Your community at launch is your first line of defense against FUD, your first source of organic distribution, and your first signal to market makers and exchanges that there is real demand.

If your Discord was created less than 30 days before launch, you do not have a community — you have an announcement channel.

## Pre-Launch Community Phases

### Phase 1: Builders (T-90 to T-60)

Who: 50 to 200 power users, developers, domain experts.
Where: Private Discord channel or Telegram group.
What: Share WIP. Get real feedback. No hype, no price talk.
Goal: Find your 10 true believers who will defend the protocol publicly.

- [ ] Hand-pick 50 people who actually understand your problem space
- [ ] Share a private build-in-public thread (a process, not a pitch)
- [ ] Run 2 to 3 live feedback sessions on product and tokenomics
- [ ] Give builders early access to testnet

### Phase 2: Early Adopters (T-60 to T-30)

Who: 500 to 2,000 qualified community members.
Where: Public Discord, Twitter/X.
What: Testnet quests, technical AMAs, educational content.
Goal: Fill the community with people who understand the product, not just the ticker.

- [ ] Open public Discord with role gating (complete a task to join main channels)
- [ ] Publish 3 to 5 long-form educational pieces (education, not marketing)
- [ ] Run a testnet/devnet program with actual rewards for feedback
- [ ] Weekly dev update: one paragraph, one screenshot, one next step

### Phase 3: Activation (T-30 to T-0)

Who: Full community.
Where: Discord, Twitter, Telegram announcement.
What: Airdrop snapshots, launch countdown, war-room prep.
Goal: Turn passive followers into active launch-day participants.

- [ ] Publish tokenomics publicly and explain every bucket
- [ ] Run a live AMA with the full founding team (video, not text)
- [ ] Publish the security audit report publicly
- [ ] Create a launch day checklist for community members (what to do, in order)
- [ ] Brief 5 to 10 KOLs with a fact sheet, not a pitch deck

## Discord Architecture (Minimal, High-Signal)

```
announcements          (admin-only)
start-here             (rules, links, role selection)
general                (gated: complete onboarding)
builders               (for devs building on the protocol)
feedback               (structured: feature requests, bugs)
governance             (when live: proposals and voting)
security               (for reporting issues — monitored 24/7)
```

Exclude: price-talk channels, speculation channels, gm/gn channels. These attract the wrong people.

**Moderation minimum:**
- 1 dedicated moderator per 1,000 members at launch
- Automod for: crypto scam links, impersonation, wallet draining scripts
- Pinned: "The team will never DM you first. Report anyone who does."

## Twitter/X Strategy

**What works pre-launch:**
- Build in public: share real progress, real numbers, real setbacks
- Technical threads: how you solved a hard problem (developers share these)
- Comparison threads: why existing solutions do not work for X
- Team credibility: individual team members tweeting authentically

**What does not work:**
- Daily countdown tweets
- "Something big is coming" teases
- Influencer paid promotion with no substance
- Price predictions or launch price speculation

**The one tweet that matters most:** publish your audit report. Tag the auditing firm. This tweet, if the audit is clean, gets more organic traction than any marketing post.

## KOL Strategy

Budget 10 to 15% of marketing budget max. Not more.

Target builders and developers with credibility in the specific domain, not generic crypto Twitter accounts with 500K followers and 0.01% engagement.

Give them a fact sheet — not a pitch — early access, and a clear ask: "please share your honest review after using it."

Red flag: any KOL who asks about "price targets" before asking about the product.

## Airdrop Design

An airdrop is a community activation tool, not a marketing stunt.

| Design principle | Why |
|---|---|
| Reward past behavior, not future promises | Attracts users who already believe in the product |
| Vesting over immediate | Reduces dump, increases community tenure |
| Activity-based over holdings-based | Protocol users over speculators |
| Transparent criteria published before snapshot | Eliminates retroactive farming accusations |

**Airdrop farmer mitigation:**
- Multi-criteria scoring (not just a single on-chain action)
- Sybil detection: cluster wallets by funding source and activity pattern
- Human verification option for borderline cases

## The Metric That Actually Matters

Discord DAU / total members at launch week should be above 15%.

Below 10%: you have bots and lurkers. You do not have a community.
Above 20%: you have an engaged community. This is rare and powerful.

Track this. It is more honest than follower count.
